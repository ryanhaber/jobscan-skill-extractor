Word Count

Many ATS use something called signal-to-noise ratio, which means that pertinent information is compared to irrelevant information.

So, using less text can actually help increase your resumes relevance. As a rule of thumb, aim for a one-page resume if you have zero to 10 years of work experience. If you have more than 10 years of work experience, you can use a two-page resumebut only if you have enough quality content to require two pages.

If you pad your resume with fluff, a hiring manager might miss important information. If you are in academia, youll use a curriculum vitae (CV) instead, which can be much longer. Many countries outside the U.S. have their own resume practicesfor example, longer resumes are the norm in Australia.

Industry Depth

Some industry specific jobs require a specialized set of skills. The more expertise or skills you have in the same field, the better your chances are. These jobs often value depth of knowledge in that industry.

If you see this tip, it means the depth of experience in your resume or LinkedIn profile aligns well with the industry-specific specialized experience in the job description.

Many recruiters recognize depth of expertise in a field as more important than having every skill from the job description.

Job Title Match

Job title is also an important factor in your match rate. Most ATS will match candidates with exact job titles to the open position.

If you had a similar position in the past, change or add that job title in your work experience to the one you are applying for. As long as your roles were essentially the same, this tactic is not deceitful. If you havent had similar job titles before, you can include it in the summary statement.

Negative words, phrases and sentiment

Hiring managers and recruiters prefer action words that define specific experiences, skills and accomplishments rather than cliched phrases, buzzwords or over-aggressive tones.

A few examples of negative words or phrases:
  • Best of Breed - Avoid cliches that does not bring value - leave the dog at home
  • Synergy - Avoid phony business jargon. Use more concrete language to convey your point more effectively.
  • Results driven - Avoid vague self-appointed adjectives. Instead, show youre results driven (or otherwise) with actual impressive results you achieved.
  • Aggressive close of deals - Avoid phrases that are overly negative in tone

Adding Measurable Results

Recruiters scanning resumes typically notice and hone in on definitive results, shown in numbers. The more specific you can be (while remaining concise), the better- add dates, length of time, and accurate numbers whenever possible.

Examples:
  • Supervised up to 25 employees
  • Reduced labor costs by more than 15%
  • Increased sales from $1.2 to $2.5 million in 2017
  • Ranked in top 5% of employee performance for 3 years running
Measurable result categories:
Money
  • sales volume
  • number of units sold
  • dollars and percentages with revenue
  • share holder value increase
  • cost savings
  • budgets or funds served
People
  • numbers of customers served
  • number of customers retained
  • number of new customers
  • numbers of direct reports
  • number of people managed or hired
  • size of teams led
Tasks
  • numbers of any tasks done in great quantity
  • efficiency increases
  • process improvements
  • time saved
Other
  • numbers of awards or publications
